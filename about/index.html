At Purdue, we strive to be the best.

Whether creating drones, designing radio transmitters, or pioneering next-gen biotech, Purdue engineers excel.

Purdue IEEE (Eye-Triple-E) is no different. Founded in 1903, we are the largest technical organization with students of every academic background. Our members work on real-world problems and advance their engineering skills.



Connecting academics with extracurriculars.

Purdue IEEE is made of Technical and Cornerstone Committees to further our goals of technical and professional growth.

Cornerstone Committees exist so all members regardless of major or prior experience have opportunities to collaborate and network with their peers, alumni, and corporate partners.

Technical Committees are each focused on a different goal, but all strive to further the technical abilities of its members by focusing on certain projects or specialty knowledge.
