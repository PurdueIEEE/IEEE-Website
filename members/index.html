Information for Members


The following information is relevant to members who utilize the Purdue IEEE SSO system. If you don't know if you are a user of the Purdue IEEE SSO system, this page may not be for you. Currently, the SSO system is utilized by Boiler Books, the ROV team, and PART.

What is the Purdue IEEE SSO system?

The Purdue IEEE SSO system is a way to unify any services committees use into a single login, with a shared account used to authenticate into multiple hosted services. You use SSO logins all the time at Purdue - BoilerKey is a type of SSO system used for Purdue University services. Here at Purdue IEEE, we use a similar system, but customized to fit our needs.

How do I create an account?

You only need to create an account if you need to use the SSO system for your committee. Once you attempt to login, you will notice a login page labeled with "Purdue IEEE SSO". Click the "Register" button on the bottom of the form to create an account. It will ask you some personal information and to confirm your email. Once you do these steps, you're done!

How do I modify any of my details?

You can view and access your account details at any time by accessing the SSO account portal. Please note that some fields, like your username, are not able to be modified. Further, modifying fields like your email may cause your account to no longer link to any data on existing services.

I'm having problems!

If you are having any issues, please reach out to the Infrastructure Lead on Slack or through email. We are here to help you! Contact us at ieee-infrastructure@purdueieee.org and someone will get back to you shortly.
